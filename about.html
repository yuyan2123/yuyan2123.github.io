My story

My story

For me, the most fascinating part of programming isn't making things work — it's making them easy to understand, easy to change, and able to live for a long time.

Ever since I wrote my first line of C in university, I've been drawn to the feeling of turning chaos into order with logic. After my CS degree I joined a startup first, doing a bit of everything — backend APIs, databases, deploy scripts — which forced me to grasp the whole development workflow quickly.

Later I moved to a mid-sized company and owned the backend of a core payments system. What I learned there went far beyond technology: when a service handles a large volume of transactions every day, any careless decision becomes someone's overtime six months later. So I started taking testing, documentation and observability seriously, and built the habit of writing technical notes.

These days I focus on backend and distributed systems, enjoy digging into low-level internals, and run self-hosted experiments that don't rely on the big cloud vendors. Outside work, I turn what I learn into articles — because explaining something clearly is how I know I truly understand it.

寫程式對我來說，最迷人的不是讓東西「能動」，而是讓它「好懂、好改、好活很久」。

我從大學接觸第一行 C 語言開始，就被那種「用邏輯把混亂收進秩序」的感覺吸引。資工系畢業後先進了一間新創，什麼都做——從後端 API、資料庫到部署腳本，逼著自己在很短的時間內補齊整個開發流程的全貌。

後來轉到一間中型企業，負責核心金流系統的後端。那段時間我學到的，遠不只是技術：當一個服務每天要處理大量交易，任何一個草率的決定，半年後都會變成別人加班的理由。於是我開始認真看待測試、文件、可觀測性，也養成了寫技術筆記的習慣。

現在的我，專注在後端與分散式系統，喜歡研究底層原理、做一些不靠雲端大廠的自架實驗。工作之外，我會把學到的東西整理成文章分享出去，因為我相信：把一件事講清楚，自己才算真的懂了。
