My story

My story

For me, the best part of programming is taking a vague idea and turning it, step by step, into something that actually runs, gets used, and solves a real problem.

My relationship with computers started in the Data Processing track at Tainan Commercial Senior High. Writing my first programs there — little Windows games in Visual Basic — hooked me: watching an idea in my head become something moving on screen was the reason I couldn't stop. Around the same time, word processing earned me a Gold Hand Award at the national vocational skills competition.

Studying Information Management at National Kaohsiung University of Science and Technology, I started pushing that curiosity toward real problems. The moment I remember most was my first year: I spent a single day writing a course-registration bot and packed my whole schedule with it — the first time I felt that code could directly change my own life. An IM background also trained me to keep one eye on the technology and one on the actual need, instead of just heads-down coding.

Now, as a master's student, I've shifted my focus to healthcare. My research lets an ordinary camera objectively assess the movements of people with Parkinson's disease — something doctors have mostly had to judge by eye, on the spot. What I really want is for that assessment to be not just objective, but simple enough for patients and their families to do at home. It's brought me into close work with neurologists at Kaohsiung Medical University Hospital, and the results already come close to the doctors' own scoring.

Beyond the thesis, I've also taken on several industry-academia projects for my advisor in smart manufacturing and image processing, working with companies and with teams from ITRI and National University of Kaohsiung to get models and algorithms running on real production lines. More than a polished demo, I care whether something holds up reliably in the real world — whether that's a clinic or a factory floor. That's the direction I want to keep building in.

對我來說，寫程式最有趣的地方，是把一個模糊的想法，一步步變成真的能跑、能用、能解決問題的東西。

我和電腦的緣分，從臺南高商資料處理科開始。那時第一次接觸程式，就著迷於用 Visual Basic 做出一個個視窗小遊戲——看著腦中的點子變成螢幕上會動的東西，是最初讓我停不下來的理由。也在那段時間，靠著文書處理拿下全國技藝競賽的金手獎。

上了高雄科技大學資訊管理系後，我開始把這份興趣往「解決真實問題」推進。印象最深的是大一，花了一天寫出選課搶課程式，把自己的課表排得滿滿——那是我第一次體會到，程式可以直接改變自己的生活。資管的訓練也讓我習慣同時看技術與需求兩邊，而不只是埋頭寫程式。

現在念碩士，我把重心轉向了醫療。我的研究讓一支普通的攝影機就能客觀評估巴金森氏症患者的動作——這件事過去多半得靠醫師當場用肉眼判斷。我真正想做到的，是讓評估不只客觀，還簡單到病患與家屬在家就能自己完成。為此，我和高雄醫學大學附設醫院神經內科的醫師密切合作，目前的結果也已經相當接近醫師的評分。

論文之外，我也接下教授的多項產學合作計畫，主題聚焦智慧製造與影像處理，和業界以及工研院、高雄大學的團隊一起，把模型與演算法落地到真正的產線情境。比起漂亮的 demo，我更在意一件事能不能穩定地在現實中運作——無論是在診間，還是在產線上。這也是我想一直做下去的方向。
